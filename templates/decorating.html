Our Baby's Bedroom

20th September 2020
23:25 BST

The time has come for us to make a start on decorating our baby's bedroom. We had this week booked off before we knew Sarah was pregnant and we had thought we might take a trip somewhere for at least a couple of days. We thought about going to Monkey World in Dorset as I loved that place. But, instead we chose to be productive before Sarah got too big. One of the many great things about Sarah is that she is very practical. She would make a great project manager!

In the two bedrooms upstairs, the walls had lining paper on them and were just painted over. Over time, the paper had started to peel off the walls so, we needed to sort it out anyway. We started pulling off the paper at the loose areas. This was actually quite time-consuming because bits of the paper would be stuck quite well. A bit like when pulling off a sticker and it leaves a layer behind (very annoying!). We had to make the areas a bit damp and use scrapping tools to scrape off the remaining bits.

After removing the remnents of the lining paper, we then started sanding down and washing the walls. The walls were in pretty bad condition (it is an old house) and I thought we may have needed to re-plaster the walls. Thankfully, we didn't have to do this and we just needed to skim the walls to put a top layer of plaster to even out the walls. Again, this turned out to be a longer process than I hoped it to be. I had to keep reminding myself that we're not professionals and we're saving a furtune. After spending a few hours skimming the walls, we left it for the evening so it can dry properly.

The next day, we started sanding down the walls to smooth them off. Sarah did a great job but, I seemed to rush my sanding too much. There were still a few lines visible but the wall felt smooth. I thought this would be alright once we put a bit of paint on it (I can already hear professionals and DIY'ers screaming at me!). Once the walls were sanded, we applied a "Pure Brilliant White" paint coat as a base. We watered down the paint 40% to help attach to the new plaster. It has been quite a hot week for a mid-September day. The walls dried pretty quickly and we had already started painting the walls our chosen colours.

We got our decoration idea from the Pinterest app and we searched for cartoon animals and safari theme. We chose "Natural Sage" as the feature wall and the other 3 walls we chose, "Champagne Cr√®me" made by Johnstone's paint. The colours are great. It didn't take long to paint and before we knew it, Sarah was hand-painting the tree and the giraffe.

Sarah is so very talented at art. I'm very proud of what we did to the room and felt we learnt a lot.
