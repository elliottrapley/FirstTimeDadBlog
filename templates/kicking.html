The First Kick

26th October 2020 09:15 GMT

Halloween is approaching. Sarah and I planned to go a Halloween-themed quiz night in a few days time at a local pub with my brother and his girlfriend. Sarah suggested we watch "Scream" from 1996. I actually hadn't seen it before but, I have seen "Scary Movie" which is a micky-take version.

After Scream had finished it was still fairly early in the evening, about 21:15. I put on "Fantastic Beasts and Where To Find Them" and we started watching that. Suddenly, Sarah grabbed my hand and place it onto her belly. She gently pressed and held my hand in place. She said, "Can you feel that?". I was dead still. I couldn't feel anything. She said, "Our son is really kicking". I kept my hand there for just a few more seconds and I felt a very slight poke. Sarah then said, "did you feel that one?", I said "Wow!, I really can".

Over the past couple of weeks, Sarah had said she can now feel the difference between a kick and movement. When I say movement I mean the baby changing position inside. When we had our 16-week scan, the sonographer said the placenta was sitting at the back of womb and Sarah may feel him sooner than others. I've tried a few times to see if I can feel the baby but unfortunately it was probably too early. It was such a surprise to feel him last night. Sarah was very sweet and got a little emotional. She's thrilled that I was able to feel our baby for the first time and, of course, so was I. It was incredible.

I can't wait to start feeling him more often. Both Sarah and I have a pregnancy app on our phone's which tells us what stage of development the baby is at. Sarah read on the app that at this stage, we could hear the baby's heartbeat using a stethoscope. I guess I now know what my next purchase is!

I know this is a relatively short post. We have our 20-week NHS scan on the 2nd November so I'll be posting another blog next week.

Until then...
